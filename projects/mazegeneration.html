About the Project

After a semester of learning different OpenGL techniques, our final was an open-ended project simply tasking us to create some graphics demonstration using whatever techniques we would like. We ended up deciding on rendering a 3D maze that you can navigate through, and we accomplished this through turning 2D matrices in code into 3D meshes, and allowing for a wide range of customized lighting and shading.

Features

  • Generating a 3D maze from a 2D matrix, allowing for navigation through the maze in player mode, or flying around in free-cam
  • Graphics settings including: light variable control, toon shading, rimlight shading, pixelation shading, dither shading, and fog
  • Dynamically switch between preset mazes during runtime

Development Process

After an even distribution of work was decided, we begun the project by rendering the maze in 2D. From there, I programmed in optimizations to remove unnecessary vertices within the maze at runtime, saving the number of drawn faces and vertices stored. After converting our 2D mazes into 3D, we developed more shading settings, added textures and movement options, and an encompassing cubemap, and finally we used Dear ImGui for our settings tab.
