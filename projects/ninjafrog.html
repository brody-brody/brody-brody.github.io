About the Project

This project was a submission for the 72-hour Mini Jam 177 hosted on itch.io. Given the theme of Papercraft, the constraint of secrets included, and a small time window, I created Ninja Frog, and enlisted my good friend Matthew O'Neill for some music in the last 24 hours. Based off of The Binding of Isaac, I created a procedurally-generated dungeon system, along with enemies and collectables, all of which randomly generated and saved location. The entire game takes place in a single Unity scene, and dungeon rooms and rock locations are loaded as you navigate through using object pooling. The dungeon generation takes an approach most similar to a breadth-first search.

https://itch.io/jam/mini-jam-177-papercraft

Features

  • Procedurally generated dungeon rooms using a breadth-first search technique
  • Random enemy and rock spawning, assisted by object pooling
  • A unique gameplay experience every time you play

Development Process

The process of Ninja Frog was most certainly not linear. A procedural dungeon system was most certainly an overscope for a 72 hour project, and the fact that I implemented it in the given time frame was a miracle, but made other parts of the project lacking, such as the overall feel of the game, and how much there was to interact with and do. I am incredibly proud of the architecture and code that I wrote, and had I been part of a larger team, I think this could have turned out amazing.

What I Learned

  • Generating a random map of rooms at run-time, and having proper transitions between them all in one scene.
  • Storing object locations and states, and loading them efficiently through object-pooling between rooms
  • Working in an incredibly time-pressured environment
