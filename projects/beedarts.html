About the Project

Bee Darts is a 3D puzzle sandbox game demo where you can throw bees like darts to interact with your surroundings. All objects are interactable, and every new object has a unique interaction.

Features

  • Hex grid map that is easily expandable through an in-editor snap-to-grid tool using axial coordinate calculations
  • Short and sweet gameplay with smooth movement and entirely random funny moments
  • Shaders, sounds, and game-feel all for good viewing and playing experience

Development Process

Starting out essentially as a joke, Bee Darts was born from a silly brainstorming activity of slapping two random words together. The bees and the first dartboards were not much of a challenge, but once I created our hex grid map, I had to create a system in which dartboards can easily control which tiles are raised from the water all from the hit of the dart. After multiple rearchitecturing processes, the rest of the game came down to how many "bits" can I make without the player being overwhelmed. Keeping code organized and efficient was a large undertaking, and development had to be made with more development in mind constantly.

What I Learned

  • A much better understanding of editor tools, like the snap-to-grid map tool, or how the map visualizes what hexagons belong to which dartboard
  • My first 'polished' 3D game, which obviously includes movement, first-person camera and sensitivity
  • In-depth into Unity-specific derived classes, as all objects derive from a dartboard base class
  • Working with axial coordinate systems to convert between a hex grid and a square grid
